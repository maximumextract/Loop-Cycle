The Problem

We collect it.
But we can't
process it.

Canadians do the right thing — they sort their recycling, put out the bin, and trust the system. But that system is fundamentally broken at the local level.

Most communities have collection infrastructure. Almost none have the knowledge, capacity, or local processing systems to actually close the loop. Plastic gets shipped elsewhere — often overseas — and much of it still ends up in landfill anyway.

BC's Recycle BC program, one of North America's first EPR systems, still only recovers 45% of plastic packaging. Victoria's 2025 pilot program ended due to lack of sustained funding — a perfect example of "Pilot Fatigue." Great initiatives start. They don't become permanent.

That gap is where Loop Cycle operates.

Who We Are

Built on purpose.
Driven by data.

"A Canada where every community has the tools and knowledge to close the loop on plastic — where recycled materials stay local, fuel local economies, and support a regenerative relationship between communities and their environment."
Our Mission
Empowering Communities
Loop Cycle empowers cities, towns, and small communities to understand, reduce, and locally process their plastic waste — building the knowledge, systems, and partnerships needed to keep plastic out of landfills and ecosystems for good.
Our Approach
Data-Driven. Community-Led.
We combine professional waste auditing methodology, municipal training programs, and the power of public art to make plastic pollution visible, measurable, and solvable — at the community level, where change is actually possible.
Our Solution

Three Pillars of Change

01
📊
Pillar One
Plastic Waste Audits
A data-driven assessment of a community's entire plastic waste stream — identifying what's being generated, where it's being lost, and what it's costing them.
  • Systematic waste stream sorting and analysis using the SWAM standard
  • Contamination identification and cost quantification
  • Targeted action plan with measurable implementation milestones
  • Annual follow-up audits to benchmark progress over time
02
🏛️
Pillar Two
Municipal Workshops
Hands-on training programs for municipal staff, teaching them to evaluate their own recycling systems, reduce contamination, and access provincial stewardship funding.
  • Train staff to conduct ongoing plastic recycling audits independently
  • Guide municipalities through RecycleBC steward registration
  • Evidence-based policy framework for circular material loops
  • School-based curriculum partnership for grades 5–9
03
🌊
Pillar Three
Public Art Installations
Transforming recovered ocean plastic into powerful public art — making pollution visible, generating public dialogue, and creating a revenue stream for local artists.
  • Partner with Ocean Legacy Foundation & Surfrider to collect marine plastics
  • Commission local artists to create large-scale public installations
  • Display at malls, civic spaces, and community venues nationally
  • Public auctions split 50/50 between artists and Loop Cycle operations
